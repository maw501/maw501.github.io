I currently work as a Lead Research Scientist in the R&D team at Faculty where I work on large language modelling and before that, probabilistic time-series forecasting. Prior to joining the R&D team I worked on a wide-range of applied machine learning projects, primarily focused on building custom algorithms for real-world business problems - this was usually for Government agencies.

Before joining Faculty I spent a long-time (too long!) working in financial markets, mostly focused on asset allocation and risk modelling. During my last few years in finance I was increasingly starting to use tools and techniques from data science and this provided a nice way for me to transition into machine learning.
